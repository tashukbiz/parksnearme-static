16:T703,Nambung National Park, located near Cervantes in Western Australia, is renowned for the Pinnacles Desert, a unique landscape featuring thousands of limestone pillars rising from yellow sands. These formations, some reaching up to 3.5 meters tall, create an otherworldly environment that attracts visitors worldwide. The park offers a 4.5 km one-way drive through the Pinnacles Desert, with designated parking bays for those wishing to explore on foot. Visitors are advised to treat the pinnacles with respect and avoid climbing on these fragile structures. The Pinnacles Desert Discovery Centre provides informative displays, soundscapes, and videos explaining the geology and cultural significance of the area. The centre also features a retail gallery and toilet facilities. Adjacent to the centre is the Pinnacles View Lookout, offering panoramic views of the desert. The park's diverse coastal landscapes include Hangover Bay, known for its wide sandy beach suitable for swimming, snorkeling, windsurfing, and surfing within the Jurien Bay Marine Park. Lake Thetis, another notable site, is home to ancient stromatolites—rock-like structures dating back to the dawn of life on Earth. The park also encompasses Kangaroo Point, an idyllic beach popular for shore fishing and boating. Facilities at Kangaroo Point include picnic tables, shade shelters, and barbecues. Entry fees apply to Nambung National Park, with standard vehicle entry at $17.00 per vehicle and concession rates available. The park is open daily, with the Pinnacles Desert Discovery Centre operating from 9:30 am to 4:30 pm, closed on Christmas Day. Visitors are encouraged to plan their visit, consider safety information, and be aware of park entry fees.
