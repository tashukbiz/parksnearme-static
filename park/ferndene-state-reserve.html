Ferndene State Reserve

Ferndene, Penguin, TAS

Ironcliffe Road, Ferndene TAS 7316

Ferndene State Reserve, located near the town of Penguin in Tasmania, offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and historical intrigue. The reserve is renowned for its well-preserved mining tunnels, including Brownings and Thorsbys Tunnels, which date back to the late 19th century. These tunnels were originally excavated for the extraction of minerals such as silver and copper. Today, they stand as a testament to the area's rich mining heritage.

The reserve features a Grade 3 walking track that meanders through a lush landscape of towering tree ferns and alongside meandering creeks, leading visitors to the historic tunnels. The track covers a distance of approximately 1.5 kilometers and involves a gentle climb of about 30 meters over 750 meters. Along the way, walkers can enjoy the soothing sounds of the burbling creek and the sight of woodland birds flitting through the trees. With some luck, one might even spot a wallaby hiding among the ferns in the dappled sunlight.

For those interested in the area's geological history, the reserve is part of the Dial Range Reserves, which showcase a diverse range of geological formations, including volcanic, dolerite, conglomerate, and sandstone layers. The region's varied geology supports a rich diversity of vegetation, from stringybark and black peppermint forests on the drier slopes to tall swamp gums and blackwood trees in the damper areas. In springtime, the understorey and heath plants burst into vibrant bloom, adding to the reserve's charm.

Visitor amenities include picnic tables and a shelter near the car park, providing a comfortable spot to relax and enjoy a meal amidst the natural surroundings. While the reserve offers a peaceful retreat, it's important to note that dogs, horses, and motorbikes are not permitted within the area to preserve its natural integrity. Additionally, campfires are prohibited, and visitors are encouraged to avoid driving in the reserves at night to protect local wildlife.

Access to the reserve is straightforward. From Ulverstone, head west towards Burnie on the A1 Bass Highway, take the South Road exit towards Penguin, then turn onto Sports Complex Avenue, followed by a left onto Ironcliffe Road. After approximately 4.6 kilometers, you'll reach Ferndene State Reserve. Limited parking is available on-site. For those planning to explore the mining tunnels, it's essential to exercise caution. These unmaintained structures are muddy, slippery, and lack lighting, so visitors should proceed at their own risk.

In summary, Ferndene State Reserve offers a captivating blend of natural beauty and historical significance, making it a must-visit destination for those interested in Tasmania's rich heritage and diverse landscapes.
