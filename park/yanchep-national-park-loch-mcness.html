Yanchep National Park (Loch McNess)

Yanchep, Perth, WA

Yanchep National Park, Yanchep WA 6035

Yanchep National Park, located approximately 50 kilometers north of Perth, is a haven for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. The park's centerpiece, Loch McNess, offers a tranquil setting for picnics and leisurely walks. The historic McNess House, built in the 1880s and now serving as a visitor center, provides insights into the park's rich heritage. Visitors can explore the park's diverse attractions, including the Koala Viewing Area, home to a colony of koalas since 1938, and the Crystal Cave, showcasing stunning stalactites and stalagmites. The park also features the Yonderup Cave, offering guided tours through its natural formations. For those interested in outdoor activities, the Yanchep Golf Course provides a scenic 9-hole experience. The park is equipped with picnic tables, shaded areas, and ample parking facilities. While dogs are not permitted, the park offers a range of activities suitable for all ages, making it a perfect destination for families and nature lovers.
