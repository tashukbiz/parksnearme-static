William Bay National Park (Greens Pool)

William Bay, Denmark, WA

William Bay Road, William Bay WA 6333

Greens Pool, located within William Bay National Park, is renowned for its tranquil turquoise waters, large granite boulders, and pristine white sandy beach. These natural features create a sheltered environment, making it an ideal spot for swimming, snorkeling, and relaxation. The calm waters are home to a diverse marine life, including zebra fish, silver drummer, six-spined leatherjackets, and mosaic sea stars, offering excellent opportunities for underwater exploration. A short walk trail connects Greens Pool to Elephant Rocks, another remarkable swimming spot in the park. Facilities at Greens Pool include picnic tables and accessible toilets, ensuring a comfortable visit for all guests. Parking is available, but during peak periods such as school and public holidays, spaces can be limited. To enhance safety, it's advisable to plan your visit during less busy times and consider traveling with a personal location beacon (PLB). Camping is not permitted within William Bay National Park. The park is jointly managed by the Denmark Shire and the Parks and Wildlife Service, and the traditional owners of the land are the Menang people.
