Ikara-Flinders Ranges National Park

Wilpena Pound, Hawker, SA

Wilpena Road, Wilpena SA 5434

Ikara-Flinders Ranges National Park is one of South Australia’s most iconic destinations, renowned for its ancient mountain landscapes, peaceful tree-lined gorges, and abundant wildlife. At its heart lies Wilpena Pound, a natural amphitheater of rugged peaks forming the park’s dramatic centrepiece. Visitors can hike to breathtaking lookouts over Wilpena Pound at sunrise or sunset, explore deep gorges like Brachina Gorge and Bunyeroo Gorge, and discover Aboriginal rock art at sites such as Arkaroo Rock. The park is rich in wildlife – you may encounter emus, kangaroos, euros, and the rare yellow-footed rock-wallaby thriving among the red rocks and cypress pines. Birdlife is plentiful as well, with wedge-tailed eagles soaring above and colorful parrots in the gum-lined valleys.

Covering approximately 95,000 hectares in the northern Flinders Ranges, the park protects a landscape of outstanding geological significance. In fact, it’s part of the Flinders Ranges UNESCO World Heritage tentative listing for its Ediacaran fossil sites, which reveal early animal life on Earth. Beyond its natural wonders, Ikara-Flinders Ranges has a rich cultural heritage – the Adnyamathanha people are the Traditional Owners, and “Ikara” (meaning “meeting place”) reflects the Indigenous name for Wilpena Pound. The park was first established in 1945 and now offers a range of visitor facilities.

Amenities include campgrounds (both powered and unpowered) at Wilpena Pound Resort and designated bush camping areas, picnic sites with shelters and BBQs, and an information centre at Wilpena Pound. There are no playgrounds here – instead the wilderness itself provides endless outdoor recreation. Adventurous travelers can tackle sections of the long-distance Heysen Trail or Mawson Trail which pass through the park, take 4WD tracks to iconic viewpoints like Razorback Lookout, or join guided tours to learn about the park’s ecology and history. Vehicle entry fees apply (park passes can be purchased online or at the visitor centre), and the park is open year-round except on days of catastrophic fire danger.

Getting there: Ikara-Flinders Ranges National Park is located about 450 km north of Adelaide, with the main entrance via Wilpena Road, 30 km northeast of the town of Hawker. The journey from Adelaide typically takes 5–6 hours by car. Once there, sealed roads lead into Wilpena Pound Resort, while many internal tracks are unsealed but accessible by 2WD in dry conditions. Accommodation: Besides campgrounds, Wilpena Pound Resort offers cabins, glamping tents, a restaurant and store, making it a convenient base.

With its awe-inspiring outback scenery, rich Aboriginal culture, hiking, camping and photography opportunities, Ikara-Flinders Ranges National Park is a must-visit for nature lovers seeking the true heart of the Australian outback.
